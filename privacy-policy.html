Privacy Policy

Scale Dynamics Limited LLC ("Company", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency about how we handle your information. This Privacy Policy explains our data practices regarding the WePray: Daily Bible Verse mobile application and related services ("Application" or "Services").

Your Privacy Matters: By downloading, installing, or using WePray, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy and consent to our data collection, processing, and usage practices as described herein.

Section 1: Data Collection Practices

1.1 Onboarding Preferences

During onboarding, WePray may ask about your Bible reading preferences, interests, and goals. This information is:

  • Stored locally on your device only
  • Never transmitted to our servers or any third party
  • Used solely to personalize your in-app experience (e.g., devotional topics, reading suggestions)

1.2 AI Bible Guide Input

When you use the AI Bible Guide feature, your chat messages are sent to OpenAI's API for processing. This occurs only:

  • After you provide explicit consent via an in-app consent dialog
  • When you actively submit a question or message in the AI Bible Guide
  • Messages are sent in real-time and are not stored on our servers

1.3 Payment Information

WePray does not collect, process, or store any payment or financial information. All subscription transactions are handled entirely by Apple through StoreKit 2 and the App Store billing system. We only receive a confirmation of subscription status from Apple.

Section 2: Automatically Collected Data

The following data is collected automatically through integrated SDKs:

Section 3: What We Do NOT Collect

Privacy-First Design: WePray is built with a privacy-first approach. We do NOT collect:
  • Name, email address, or phone number
  • GPS location or precise geolocation data
  • Health data or biometric information
  • Contacts, photos, or files from your device
  • Browsing history outside the app

No account creation is required. All reading progress, streaks, devotional preferences, and bookmarks are stored locally on your device. No chat history is stored on our servers -- AI conversations exist only during the active session.

Section 4: How We Use Data

4.1 Service Delivery and Improvement

We use automatically collected data to:

  • Understand which features are most valuable to users
  • Identify and fix bugs, crashes, and performance issues
  • Guide product development and feature prioritization
  • Optimize the onboarding experience

4.2 Marketing Attribution

We use Adjust solely to measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns by understanding where app installs originate. We do not use this data for targeted advertising, retargeting, or selling to advertisers.

4.3 AI Service Delivery

Chat messages sent to OpenAI are used solely to generate real-time responses to your Bible questions. We do not use your chat messages for training, analytics, or any other purpose.

Section 6: AI Disclosure

6.1 How AI Works in WePray

The AI Bible Guide is powered by OpenAI's language models. When you submit a question:

  • Your message is sent to OpenAI's API over an encrypted connection
  • OpenAI processes the message and returns a response
  • The conversation is displayed in the app in real-time

6.2 Consent

Before your first use of the AI Bible Guide, a consent dialog explains that your messages will be sent to OpenAI. You must accept this before the feature becomes available. You can choose not to use the AI Bible Guide, and all other app features will remain fully functional.

6.3 Data Retention by OpenAI

WePray does not store your chat messages on any server. OpenAI's data retention and processing practices are governed by OpenAI's own privacy policy and API data usage policies. As of our integration, OpenAI's API does not use submitted data for training purposes.

Section 7: App Tracking Transparency (ATT)

7.1 IDFA Collection

WePray requests access to your device's Identifier for Advertisers (IDFA) through Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) framework. This prompt appears when you first open the app.

7.2 Purpose

The IDFA is used exclusively for Adjust attribution measurement -- to understand which marketing campaigns drive app installs. It is:

  • NOT used for advertising or ad targeting (WePray contains no ads)
  • NOT shared with advertising networks
  • NOT used for cross-app tracking or user profiling

7.3 Declining ATT

If you decline the ATT prompt, the app functions identically. No IDFA is collected, and attribution measurement falls back to privacy-friendly methods. Your experience is not affected in any way.

Section 9: Data Security

9.1 Technical Safeguards

We implement appropriate security measures including:

  • Encryption in Transit: All network communications use TLS encryption
  • API Key Security: Third-party API keys are securely managed and never exposed in client-side code
  • Minimal Data Collection: We collect only what is necessary for the app to function
  • Local-First Architecture: Sensitive user data (preferences, reading history) never leaves your device

9.2 Data Breach Response

In the unlikely event of a data security incident affecting user data, we commit to:

  • Immediate containment and assessment of the breach
  • Notification to affected users within 72 hours
  • Cooperation with relevant regulatory authorities
  • Implementation of additional safeguards to prevent recurrence

Section 10: User Rights

10.1 All Users

Regardless of your location, you have the right to:

  • Decline the ATT prompt and opt out of IDFA collection
  • Delete all local data by uninstalling the app
  • Choose not to use the AI Bible Guide feature
  • Request information about what data we hold (contact us via email)
  • Limit analytics collection through iOS device settings (Settings > Privacy > Analytics)

10.2 California Residents (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to Know: Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information we hold about you
  • Right to Opt-Out: WePray does not sell personal information to third parties
  • Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights

10.3 European Residents (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right of Access: Obtain a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate personal data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the email address below.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child under 13 has used WePray and provided personal information, please contact us immediately so we can take appropriate action.
